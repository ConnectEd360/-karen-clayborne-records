02 Clinical Summary

Karen Clayborne is a 60-year-old never-smoking female with Stage IV non-small cell lung adenocarcinoma (EGFR Exon 19 deletion) diagnosed in October 2024 following evaluation of a persistent cough. Initial CT chest showed a dominant right hilar mass (8.8 × 7.5 × 7.7 cm) with pleural effusion, T3 and T9 vertebral lesions, and liver hypodensities. Thoracentesis confirmed malignant cells consistent with lung primary adenocarcinoma.

She was initially treated with amivantamab + lazertinib (November 2024, patient-preferred chemo-free regimen, Dr. Krishna Alluri, Texas Oncology). Systemic response was partial — CT TAP February 2025 showed partial response. However, she subsequently developed CNS metastasis, discovered February–March 2025, with a dominant cerebellar vermis lesion measuring 3.1 × 2.4 × 3.0 cm with 4th ventricle effacement. The April 1–3, 2025 hospitalization at Northeast Baptist formally confirmed the lesion and initiated SRS planning — CCD pg 1–50 pg 51–100 pg 101–128

SRS Course 1 (30 Gy / 5 fractions, April–May 2025, TXO / Dr. Dzuick) produced an excellent initial response — lesion reduced to 1.0 × 0.6 × 1.0 cm by June 2025. However, regrowth was confirmed by August 2025 (1.4 cm), with further progression to 1.9 × 1.7 cm by November 2025 and a new right frontal mass (0.7 × 0.8 cm)* appearing.

In May 2025, she was transitioned to osimertinib (Tagrisso) 80 mg daily after declining rechallenge with lazertinib and amivantamab. Of particular note, TEMPUS and Guardant360 testing confirmed a germline BRCA2 variant (F2560fs, VAF 48.8%) — an uncommon co-mutation in EGFR-driven lung adenocarcinoma that may carry independent therapeutic relevance, including potential eligibility for PARP inhibitor combinations or BRCA2-directed clinical trials not available in the community setting. Genetic counseling and family testing have been recommended but not yet completed. She has also had a DVT (peroneal vein, May 2025), pulmonary embolism (May 2025), IJ occlusive DVT (June 2025), and recurrent bilateral PE (March 2026), for which she remains on lifelong apixaban.

SRS Course 2 (27 Gy / 3 fractions, February 4–6, 2026, Dr. Dalwadi, Mays Cancer Center) targeted the left frontal lesion only. The dominant vermis lesion was deliberately excluded from treatment per neuro-oncology tumor board decision on February 6, 2026, with a plan to watch. The left frontal lesion has since responded well (↓ to 9 mm by April 10, 2026).

The dominant cerebellar vermis lesion, which has never received definitive treatment at full dose, has continued to progress: 2.2 × 2.6 × 2.2 cm as of April 10, 2026 (last formal measurement), with confirmed further growth on May 24, 2026 ER MRI. A second cerebellar mass (left lower cerebellum) measures 1.6 × 2.1 cm. Multiple additional subcentimeter lesions are present bilaterally. The patient is now admitted with acute dysarthria, dysphagia, left-sided weakness, and ataxia — all consistent with active posterior fossa mass effect.

Staging on May 25, 2026 (MRI C/T/L spine + CT chest/abdomen/pelvis) confirmed no active spinal or systemic metastases. Prior T9 and L3 changes represent chronic/treated disease. The right lung primary shows post-treatment changes without recurrence. The disease burden is now entirely CNS-dominant, making CNS-directed decision-making the immediate priority.

⚠ Leptomeningeal Disease — Newly Identified: On May 25, 2026, Dr. Jason Boulter MD (Neurosurgery) identified enhancement extending in a spiculated pattern along the cerebellar folia from the dominant vermis lesion on independent review of the May 24 MRI Brain — findings consistent with leptomeningeal disease. This finding is not documented in the radiologist's written impression (Dr. David Wood, signed 05/24/2026). Dr. Boulter explicitly cited this finding as the primary basis for excluding surgical candidacy, stating that resection "would offer little to no meaningful survival benefit" given the leptomeningeal extension. Dedicated leptomeningeal workup — including lumbar puncture and CSF cytology — has not been performed. This finding should be formally confirmed and considered in all treatment planning discussions.

Multiple providers have formally documented concern about care continuity across multiple institutions. Family is engaged and supportive. As of 05/26/2026, Carol Clayborne Porter serves as surrogate decision-maker. Importantly, Karen was KPS 90 as recently as April 14, 2026 — six weeks before this admission — indicating that her functional decline is acute and CNS-driven, not a reflection of underlying systemic deterioration. At 60 years old with no active systemic disease, she remains a reasonable candidate for aggressive CNS-directed intervention.
